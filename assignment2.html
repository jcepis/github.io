but whatever you do,

you have to keep moving forward." - Marting Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. born in Atlanta, Georgia on January 15, 1929. He was assasinated in 1968. A Babist minister, he was also a Civil-Rights Activist, and led the Civil-Rights Movment. His activism changed society, making legal racial segregation a thing of the past. Mr. King strived for what he thought was right for the betterment of the people, to move forward. In 1963, he gave his "I have a dream." speech which he often referenced to. His actions led to the Civil Rights Act in 1964. In the same year, he was given a Nobel Peace prize, as well as other honors. In the following year, he also led to the Voting Act in 1955 being passed as well.
